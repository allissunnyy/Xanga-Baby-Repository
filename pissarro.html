Camille Pissarro

Camille Pissarro (1830–1903)

The eldest of the Impressionists, Pissarro was a mentor figure who exhibited in all eight Impressionist shows. His landscapes captured both rural tranquility and urban bustle with atmospheric sensitivity.

Key Works

Legacy

Pissarro bridged Impressionism and Pointillism, always prioritizing natural light and harmonious color over dramatic effect.
